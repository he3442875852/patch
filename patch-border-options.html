Edges affect durability and appearance

Patch Border Options

The border controls how the patch edge looks, how it handles curves and small details, and how durable the finished patch feels. Merrow, laser cut, and hot cut are common choices for custom patches.

Ask About Border OptionsChoose Backing

Merrow Border

A traditional stitched edge that creates a raised rope-like border around the patch. It works best on simple circles, ovals, rectangles, shields, and classic embroidered patches.

Laser Cut Edge

A clean cut edge for custom shapes, detailed outlines, woven labels, printed patches, and designs that need a precise silhouette without a thick raised border.

Hot Cut Edge

A heat-sealed edge used for many embroidered, woven, and printed patches. It helps reduce fraying and supports more flexible shapes than a merrow border.

Stitched Border

A flat stitched border follows the design edge without the heavy rope look. It can be useful when the artwork needs a clean but softer finish.

Chenille Border

Chenille patches often use felt layers plus embroidered outlines. This gives varsity patches a soft raised texture and bold edge definition.

PVC Raised Edge

PVC patches can use a raised molded edge or recessed outline. This creates a durable rubber border that protects the design and adds dimension.

Quick Border Guide

  • Classic embroidered badge: merrow border.
  • Custom shape or fine outline: laser cut or hot cut.
  • Woven label with small text: laser cut edge.
  • Printed patch with full-color artwork: heat-cut or laser-cut edge.
  • PVC patch: molded raised edge.
  • Chenille letter: felt base with embroidered outline.
When should I choose merrow border?

Choose merrow border when the patch has a simple shape and you want a classic raised stitched edge. It is common for round, oval, square, shield, and rectangular patches.

What is the difference between laser cut and hot cut?

Both can create cleaner custom shapes than merrow. Laser cut is often used for precision and detailed silhouettes, while hot cut uses heat to seal fabric edges and reduce fraying.

Can every patch use merrow border?

No. Complex shapes, sharp corners, small details, and narrow cutouts often work better with laser cut or hot cut edges.

Does border choice affect price?

It can. Border type, shape complexity, patch size, and material all affect production time and quote details.
