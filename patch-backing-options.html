Patch Backing Options

Backing affects how a custom patch is applied, removed, washed, packed, and priced. Choose the backing based on garment, use case, durability, and whether the patch needs to be permanent or removable.

Request Backing AdviceCompare Borders

Sew-On Backing

The most durable option for jackets, uniforms, bags, denim, hats, and long-term wear. It is recommended when the patch must survive repeated washing or heavy use.

Iron-On Backing

A heat-activated backing for convenient application on many fabrics. It is useful for apparel and light merchandise, but sewing is still recommended for high-wear items.

Hook and Loop

Also called velcro backing. Best for removable morale patches, tactical patches, uniforms, bags, gear, and name patches that need to be swapped.

Self-Adhesive

A peel-and-stick backing for temporary use, packaging, displays, event kits, and short-term decoration. It is not the best choice for washing or long-term garments.

Pin Backing

Good for badges, events, staff access, retail display, and removable wear. Pin backing can work well when the customer does not want to sew or heat-press.

No Backing

Used when a garment factory or sewing team will apply the patch during production. This keeps the patch flexible and ready for industrial sewing.

How to Choose Backing

  • Uniform or jacket: sew-on or hook and loop.
  • Hat patch: sew-on, adhesive for certain leather patches, or heat application depending on material.
  • Outdoor gear: hook and loop or sew-on.
  • Event giveaway: iron-on, pin, or self-adhesive.
  • Retail apparel: sew-on for durability and a premium finish.
Is iron-on backing permanent?

Iron-on backing can hold well on suitable fabric, but heat, washing, fabric texture, and use conditions matter. For long-term wear, sew around the edge after heat application.

What is hook and loop backing?

Hook and loop backing uses two mating surfaces so a patch can be attached and removed. It is common for tactical, morale, uniform, and gear patches.

Can PVC patches use backing?

Yes. PVC patches commonly use hook and loop, sew channels, adhesive, or magnetic/pin style attachments depending on the use case.

Which backing should I request?

Send your product type, fabric, washing needs, and whether the patch must be removable. We can recommend a backing option based on those details.
