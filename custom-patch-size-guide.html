Custom Patch Size Guide

Patch size affects readability, stitch detail, backing choice, packaging, and cost. Choose a size based on where the patch will be used and how much detail the artwork contains.

2-3 inch patches

Common for hats, sleeves, small badges, labels, and simple logos. Keep text bold and avoid tiny details.

3-4 inch patches

A flexible size for uniforms, clubs, sports teams, bags, jackets, and event patches with moderate detail.

4-6 inch patches

Good for jacket fronts, back packs, large logos, motorcycle patches, and artwork that needs more visual impact.

Large back patches

Used for jackets, vests, clubs, and statement apparel. Large patches need careful backing and border planning.

Small text

Very small letters may not stitch cleanly. Woven or printed patches are often better for fine text and detailed marks.

Custom shapes

Irregular shapes should allow enough edge space for cutting, stitching, or molded borders depending on material.

Common Size Uses

  • Hat front patch: often 2 to 3.5 inches wide.
  • Uniform shoulder patch: often 3 to 4 inches.
  • Jacket chest patch: often 3 to 5 inches.
  • Back patch: usually larger and quoted by artwork.
  • Woven apparel label: small sizes can work well with fine detail.
How do I measure patch size?

Most patch sizes are quoted by width and height at the widest points. For irregular shapes, send artwork so the outline can be measured accurately.

Does bigger always mean better detail?

Bigger patches allow more detail, but material matters too. Embroidery has thread limitations, while woven and printed patches can handle finer artwork.

Can you suggest a size?

Yes. Send the product type, artwork, and where the patch will be placed. We can recommend a practical size range.

Does size affect backing?

Yes. Large patches may need sew-on or strong attachment methods, while small promotional patches may use adhesive, pin, or iron-on backing.
